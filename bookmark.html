How to Use Bookmarks

What are Bookmarks?

Bookmarks are a way to save your favorite web pages for easy access later. They allow you to quickly return to sites you visit frequently.

How to Bookmark a Page

  • Google Chrome: Click the star icon in the address bar or press Ctrl + D (Windows) or Cmd + D (Mac).
  • Mozilla Firefox: Click the star icon in the address bar or press Ctrl + D (Windows) or Cmd + D (Mac).
  • Safari: Click the share button and select "Add Bookmark" or press Cmd + D.
  • Microsoft Edge: Click the star icon in the address bar or press Ctrl + D.

How to Access Your Bookmarks

  • Google Chrome: Click the three dots in the top right corner, then select "Bookmarks".
  • Mozilla Firefox: Click the library icon (bookshelf) in the top right corner, then select "Bookmarks".
  • Safari: Click the bookmarks icon in the toolbar or go to "Bookmarks" in the menu bar.
  • Microsoft Edge: Click the three dots in the top right corner, then select "Favorites".

Organizing Your Bookmarks

You can create folders to organize your bookmarks. Most browsers allow you to drag and drop bookmarks into folders for better organization.
